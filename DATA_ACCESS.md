# Data Access Guide

This document provides detailed instructions for obtaining the restricted-access data necessary to replicate the analysis in "Gender-Directed Sector-Specific Technical Change."

---

## Overview

The empirical analysis requires **two types of data**:

1. **Publicly available:** Productivity statistics (immediately accessible)
2. **Restricted access:** Swiss Federal Statistical Office (FSO) microdata (requires application)

---

## Publicly Available Data

### Productivity Statistics (Wachstums- und Produktivitätsstatistik)

**How to access:**

1. Visit the Swiss Federal Statistical Office website: https://www.bfs.admin.ch/
2. Navigate to: *Themen* → *Volkswirtschaft* → *Volkswirtschaftliche Gesamtrechnung* → *Wachstum und Produktivität*
3. Download annual data for years 2012–2020
4. Required variables:
   - Sectoral productivity (value added per worker)
   - NOGA 2008 sector classification codes

**No application required** — data are freely downloadable.

**Data format:** Excel/CSV files  
**Documentation:** Methodological reports available on BFS website

---

## Restricted-Access Data (FSO Microdata)

### Required Datasets

**1. Swiss Earnings Structure Survey (Lohnstrukturerhebung, LSE)**

**Coverage:**
- Survey years: 2012, 2014, 2016, 2018, 2020
- Approximately 1.6–2.1 million employee observations per wave
- Representative sample of Swiss labor market

**Key variables used:**
- Individual earnings (monthly gross wage)
- Hours worked (weekly hours, work time percentage)
- Occupation codes (ISCO-08)
- Sector classification (NOGA 2008)
- Gender
- Region (canton, Grossregion)
- Employment type (full-time/part-time)

**2. Central Compensation Office (ZAS) Administrative Wage Data**

**Coverage:**
- Years: 2012–2020
- Administrative records from social security system

**Key variables used:**
- Standardized gross monthly wages
- Employment status verification
- Used for data validation and plausibility checks

## Data Access Procedure

### Overview

Access to linked FSO microdata for third-party research projects follows a 
standardized application process managed by BFS. 
Full details are available at: 
https://www.bfs.admin.ch/bfs/de/home/dienstleistungen/datenverknuepfungen/fuer-dritte.html

### Application Process

#### Step 1: Contact Relevant Section Offices (Fachsektionen)

**Before submitting your application**, you must contact the subject matter 
sections (Fachsektionen) responsible for the datasets you need:

**For Labor Market Data (LSE, ZAS):**
```
Sektion Arbeit und Erwerbsleben
Email: arbeit-erwerb@bfs.admin.ch
```

**For Population Data (STATPOP):**
```
Sektion Demografie und Migration
Email: info.dem@bfs.admin.ch
```

**Purpose of this contact:**
- Clarify exactly which variables you need from each dataset
- Confirm data availability for your research period
- Finalize the "data requirement section" of your application form (Antragsformular)
- Get technical guidance on feasibility

**Timeline:** Allow 2-4 weeks for this preliminary consultation and keep
emails as confirmation of this guided research.

---

#### Step 2: Prepare Application Documents

Once you have clarified your data requirements with the section offices, prepare:

**1. Application Form (Antragsformular für Datenverknüpfung)**
Download from: https://www.bfs.admin.ch/bfs/de/home/dienstleistungen/datenverknuepfungen/fuer-dritte.html

**2. Data Protection Concept (Datenschutzkonzept)**
Template available on BFS website

**3. Research Proposal**
5-10 page summary

**4. Institutional Support Letter**
From your university/research institution

**5. CV of Principal Investigator(s)**

**6. Funding Confirmation (if applicable)**

---

#### Step 3: Submit Application

**Timeline:** 
- Initial review: 4-8 weeks
- Additional information requests: 2-4 weeks (if needed)
- Final decision: 3-6 months total from submission to approval

---

#### Step 4: Contract Negotiation

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks for contract finalization

---

#### Step 5: Data Access

**Remote Secure Access:**
Data provided remotely via "filetransfer" by FSO

---

#### Costs

**Fee waivers:**
Fee may be reduced or waived for research projects

### Important Notes

**Data Protection Compliance:**
- Switzerland has strict data protection laws (nDSG, VDSZ)
- BFS operates under federal statistical confidentiality obligations
- Violations can result in:
  - Contract termination
  - Criminal penalties (up to 3 years imprisonment or fine)
  - Institutional sanctions

**Publication Requirements:**
- All outputs must be cleared by BFS before publication
- Minimum cell sizes apply (typically ≥10 observations)
- Individual-level information must never be disclosed
- Proper data source citation required

**Contract Renewal:**
- Can request extension before expiration
- Requires justification for continued need
- Additional fees may apply

---

### Timeline Summary

**Total from first contact to data access:** 6-12 months

**Planning tip:** Start the process 12+ months before you need the data for analysis.

## Alternative Approaches for Researchers Without Data Access

If you cannot obtain FSO microdata, you may:

1. **Use publicly available aggregates:**
   - FSO publishes some aggregated wage statistics by sector/gender
   - Limited regional detail and no individual controls
   - May allow qualitative validation of main findings
   - Available at: https://www.bfs.admin.ch/bfs/de/home/statistiken/arbeit-erwerb/loehne-erwerbseinkommen-arbeitskosten.html

2. **Contact authors for aggregated results:**
   - We may provide additional aggregated tables/statistics not in the paper
   - Subject to FSO confidentiality clearance
